Team Charter
Regular Meeting Times

All members of Team Superior have committed to two in-person group meetings; Thursday at 11:00 am to 12:00 pm (location BA2175), Monday at 3:00 pm to 4:00 pm (location BA3200).

Along with these two in-person meetings, we have also agreed on a modified daily stand-up meeting; modified meaning we meet via messenger (WhatsApp) daily for fifteen minutes from 12:00 pm to 12:15 pm.

Values

Our group's main values consist of shared learning and curiosity. We share a common understanding of education: it is more than an integer (our grades). Education is about choosing to learn something new, and sharing that knowledge amongst those around you. It is not looking for the easy way out, but following curiosity into a new domain. You may come to hate your new found knowledge, but at least you'll hate it together (curiosity may have killed the cat, but it would never take on a clowder). This is why our group has chosen to use Ruby on Rails; a language all members are using for the first time.

Communication, honesty, and respect are the key elements of a functional relationship with any group of any size. Our group abides by the values of honesty, respect and communication. This allows us to build a relationship that either excels in success, or falls in failure, as a unit. We respect each other enough to be able to voice out any need for, or offer of, help to one another. We will not silently fail out of fear of being ostracized from the group; in contrast, any problems brought forth will be seen as an opportunity for growth for all members.

It may seem odd to group these together but alas; diligence, and community. Diligence is written last, not because it is any less of a value for our group, but so that it is understood that asking for help when in doubt is a part of being diligent. All members understand that although a task may have a name assigned to it, each member is responsible for helping one another succeed; this is what brings about our group’s sense of community (it is your task, but our project).

Commitments

Our primary communication avenues are E-mail and text message. We each commit to respond to a text within one hour (three hours the latest), and respond to emails that require a response by the end of the day.

In addition, we all have agreed upon visiting the Team’s facebook group at least once a day, at any time convenient.

If a member cannot make it to either a daily meeting, or an in-person meeting, they must give a twenty-four hour notice via email stating the reason for their absence. Depending on the importance of the meeting, the rest of the members can choose to conduct the meeting without the member in question, or change the time-slot for that particular meeting to accommodate them.

Team Charter

We are building a web-based membership and program registration system for the North York Seniors Centre. Currently, membership and class registration is done in pen and paper at the Centre, and all client records are kept in a paper filing cabinet. This project aims to provide more convenience to both those who participate at NYSC as well as those that work there.

Our system will allow members to renew their memberships and register for classes online. Non-members must also be able to create new memberships as well as register for classes. Registrations done online should be handled in a secure manner via Paypal. Registrations should still be able to be processed and paid in person, by having a staff person or volunteer add the user into the database.

When complete, NYSC should be able to redirect clients who are interested in registering for programs and membership to the online registration system. Registrants will no longer be forced to wait in long lines to secure spots in their favourite classes. The Centre will no longer have to allocate a large amount of time and resources for volunteers and workers to manually process these forms. Allowing online registration will also increase the saturation of the Centre’s target market; as such, the Centre should see a trend in the short-to-long term of an overall increase in activity and participation post-launch of the online system. Finally, Centre staff should report that a digitized client management system helps paper-saving, as well as greater ease with regards to generating and analyzing aggregate client statistics.

Technologies to be used

The web application will be developed with the Ruby on Rails framework, which we have collectively chosen out of personal interest, motivated by a desire to all learn and become familiar with a new technology. All of our source code and documentation will be version controlled on GitHub, with our GitHub project webpage used as the main medium in sharing our work and progress. The GitHub pages feature is easy to use, publicly accessible (should we later choose to feature our work in our individual portfolios), and most of all makes it easy for those who may not be familiar with GitHub itself to easily gain a high-level understanding of the project.

For the database, we will be employing sqlite3, which comes by default with Rails. The web server is currently being hosted with an Ubuntu VM on Windows Azure, and we plan to integrate an Apache server in the near future.
